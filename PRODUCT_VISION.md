# Daylight Dashboard Product Vision

## Product

Daylight Dashboard is a family companion platform for loved ones experiencing dementia, memory loss, aging-related confusion, sundowning, isolation, or major life transitions.

The name is intentional: daylight means clarity, calm, reassurance, warmth, orientation, and connection. It is a gentle counterpoint to sundowning without making the product feel clinical or frightening.

## Positioning

Daylight Dashboard is not primarily a dementia app, a medical tracker, a surveillance tool, or a diagnosis platform.

It is:

- a family companion platform
- a daily reassurance system
- a memory support dashboard
- a family connection hub
- a care circle coordination tool
- a legacy and story preservation platform
- a practical family continuity system

The product should preserve dignity, autonomy, identity, connection, and emotional safety.

## First Use Case

The first real user is Mary. Mary is 82 and experiences memory loss and confusion. The first goal is to help Mary feel calm, safe, oriented, loved, less guilty, less responsible for remembering everything, and connected to her family.

Mary’s view should be extremely simple: large text, calm colors, minimal navigation, no clutter, no technical language, no medical language, no AI language, and no scary language.

The page should answer:

1. What day is it?
2. Where am I?
3. What is happening today?
4. What has already been taken care of?
5. Who loves me?
6. Who has checked on me recently?
7. What is coming up?
8. What do I not need to worry about?

## Mary-Safe Language

Never show Mary language like:

- dementia
- memory loss
- cognitive decline
- confused
- paranoid
- repeated question
- you forgot
- you already asked
- you were wrong
- overdue
- missed

Use instead:

- here’s what’s true today
- this has already been taken care of
- your family is helping
- Erin checked in recently
- Richard is nearby
- you are safe
- you are loved
- you do not need to worry about this today

Raw/admin notes can contain more detail, but Mary’s view must always be Mary-safe.

## MVP Sections

Build or preserve these sections first:

- today’s date/day/time
- warm greeting
- daily reassurance
- today’s simple plan
- recently completed / already handled
- recent conversations
- family check-ins
- upcoming events/countdowns
- birthdays
- anniversaries
- holidays
- fun national days
- grocery/fridge snapshot
- journal reminder
- things that are true
- where things are
- family photos
- message from family
- comfort/reassurance mode

## Family/Admin Experience

Family admins should be able to quickly update the dashboard. Erin or Richard should be able to add a recent conversation, family check-in, upcoming event, grocery/fridge update, reassurance card, completed item, journal reminder, family photo, voice message later, and memory/story later.

Manual admin updates are acceptable for the MVP. Email ingestion and AI approval can come after the first usable deployment.

## Nonprofit Vision

The for-profit company is Daylight Dashboard at `DaylightDashboard.com`.

There may also be a nonprofit arm to provide free or sponsored access for families who need support and cannot afford a subscription. A strong candidate name is The Daylight Initiative.

Potential mission: provide free access to connection, memory support, reassurance, and family coordination tools for people and families affected by dementia, Alzheimer’s, memory loss, aging-related confusion, and isolation.

Potential donor model: sponsor one family for one year.

## MVP Priority

Do not overbuild.

1. Get Mary’s dashboard live.
2. Make it beautiful, simple, and usable.
3. Add admin forms so Erin/Richard can update it.
4. Add countdowns and holidays.
5. Add family photos and recent conversations.
6. Add comfort/reassurance mode.
7. Deploy to the web.
8. Observe what Mary actually uses.
9. Improve based on real family use.

Do not build billing, full SaaS, Alexa, facility tools, or advanced AI before the MVP is live and tested.

## Development Philosophy

Working software over perfect architecture.

Human reassurance over feature count.

Manual first, automation later.

Mary-safe content over raw accuracy.

Dignity over monitoring.

Connection over complexity.

The product should never make Mary feel managed. It should make her feel loved.
